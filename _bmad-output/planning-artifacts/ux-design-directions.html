Typerek 2.0 ‚Äî Design Directions

Design Direction Evaluation Guide
Use these criteria to evaluate which direction feels right for typerek 2.0

Layout Intuitiveness

Which information hierarchy matches the "check leaderboard ‚Üí place bets" daily ritual?

Betting Speed

Which layout makes inline multi-match betting fastest? Fewest taps, clearest targets.

Non-Technical User Friendliness

Which would your father find easiest to navigate? Largest targets, clearest labels.

Information Density

Which balances "see everything at a glance" with "not overwhelming"?

Match State Clarity

Which makes open/locked/scored states most immediately obvious?

Social Reveal Moment

Which makes the kickoff bet reveal feel like a shared social event?

Direction Summary

A ‚Äî Bordered Cards

Clean card boundaries with subtle borders. Each match is a clear unit. Structured and orderly. Best for: users who like clear separation between items.

B ‚Äî Elevated Cards

Floating cards with soft shadows. Feels more modern and layered. Slight depth gives visual hierarchy. Best for: a polished, app-like feel.

C ‚Äî Full-Width Rows

No card boundaries ‚Äî matches flow as continuous list rows. Maximum density, fastest scanning. Best for: power users who want to scroll and bet quickly.

D ‚Äî Compact Density

Tighter spacing, smaller elements. More matches visible at once. Best for: showing many matches on screen, experienced users.

E ‚Äî Accent Header

Teal header bar gives strong brand presence. Content area stays white and clean below. Best for: clear visual identity and app branding.

F ‚Äî Pill Buttons

Rounded pill-shaped bet buttons instead of rounded rectangles. Softer, more playful feel. Best for: friendlier, less formal interaction style.
